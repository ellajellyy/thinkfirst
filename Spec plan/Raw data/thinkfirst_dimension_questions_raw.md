## Area 1 — WHO HAS THIS PROBLEM (user specificity)

### What the KB says a builder must know

- The overarching mistake in startups is "not making something users want." Mistake #10 is explicitly "Having No Specific User in Mind," stressing the need to understand who the users are and their problems, especially if the founders are not the target market. [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/paul-graham-ycombinator/The 18 mistakes that kill startups.md`]

- "If you start too generic, everything is watered down. Your marketing message is generic. You suffer feature creep. Google helped PhD students find obscure bits of code. eBay helped collectors buy and sell Pez dispensers. Evernote helped moms save and share recipes." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "When you have a fuzzy sense of who you're serving, you end up talking to a lot of different types of people, which leads to confusing signals and three problems: 1. You get overwhelmed by options and don't know where to start; 2. You aren't moving forward but can't prove yourself wrong; 3. You receive incredibly mixed feedback and can't make sense of it." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Before we can serve everyone, we have to serve someone." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "If you aren't finding consistent problems and goals, you don't yet have a specific enough customer segment." [Rule of thumb] [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Good customer segments are a who-where pair. If you don't know where to go to find your customers, keep slicing your segment into smaller pieces until you do." [Rule of thumb] [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "They weren't having 20 conversations with their customers. They were having one conversation each with 20 different types of customers. That's why the feedback was so inconsistent. It was as if they were exploring two dozen business ideas simultaneously." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Don't just talk to the most senior or important people you can find. You want to talk to people who are representative of your customers, not ones who sound impressive on your status report." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Founder market fit" is named as the most important criterion for evaluating a startup idea: "Are you the right team to be working on this idea?" [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/yc-startup-school/How to Get and Evaluate Startup Ideas.md`]

- "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas. It's to look for problems, preferably problems you yourself have." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/paul-graham-ycombinator/How to get start up ideas.md`]

## Area 2 — IS THE PROBLEM REAL (problem reality)

### What the KB says a builder must know

- "The Mom Test: 1. Talk about their life instead of your idea. 2. Ask about specifics in the past instead of generics or opinions about the future. 3. Talk less and listen more." — the core framework for obtaining evidence of real vs assumed problems. [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Opinions are worthless." Compliments, "sounds interesting," and hypothetical agreements are explicitly named as bad data. Past behaviours are the only reliable evidence. [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Most people have lots of problems which they don't actually care enough about to fix, but which they'll happily tell you the details of if you ask them. Before you have solid evidence that you're fixing a meaningful problem for your customer segment, you can really mess yourself up by zooming in too quickly." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Anchoring fluff: generic claims, future promises, hypotheticals — these are all fluff and must be anchored to past specifics." The book describes three types of fluff: generic claims ("I usually," "I always"), future-tense promises ("I would," "I will"), and hypotheticals ("If I had," "If the product did X"). [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Great founders are almost always talking to users. Users keep founders honest." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/yc-startup-school/How to Talk to Users.md`]

- "You're searching for the truth, not trying to be right. Learning that your beliefs are wrong is frustrating, but it's progress. It's bringing you ever closer to the truth of a real problem and a good market." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]

- "Would you use this yourself, if you hadn't built it?" — named as a key test for whether the founder themselves recognizes real value from their own product. [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/paul-graham-ycombinator/What I Learned from Users.md`]

- "The way to get startup ideas is to look for problems, preferably problems you yourself have. The most reliable source of good startup ideas is noticing problems you yourself have." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/paul-graham-ycombinator/How to get start up ideas.md`]

- Good problems are acute — "good problems have no alternative or current solutions suck." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/ella@second_brain/ella@llm_wiki/raw/yc-startup-school/How to Get and Evaluate Startup Ideas.md`]

- "Dig beneath ideas" — when someone makes a feature request or expresses interest, the real signal is the underlying problem or motivation, not the stated request itself. "What you should do in this situation is to understand the underlying goal." [`/Users/anlalalaa_/Projects/AI PM/thinkfirst/Knowledge base/raw/the-mom-test-by-rob-fitzpatrick.md`]
