## Summary

Motivational Interviewing (MI) is a **collaborative, goal-oriented** communication style that strengthens motivation and commitment to a goal by eliciting the person’s own reasons for change in an atmosphere of acceptance and compassion.

Core themes across the documents:\n
- MI is a **guiding** style (between following and directing).\n
- It focuses on **ambivalence** and resolves it by evoking the client’s own motivations.\n
- It uses OARS skills (open questions, affirmations, reflections, summaries).\n
- It emphasizes autonomy: avoid unsolicited advice, confrontation, or coercion.\n

---

## Key frameworks

### MI “spirit”

Partnership, evocation, acceptance, compassion.

### Processes

Engaging → focusing → evoking → (optional) planning.

### Practical micro-techniques

Elicit–Provide–Elicit for information exchange; reflective listening to reduce resistance.
