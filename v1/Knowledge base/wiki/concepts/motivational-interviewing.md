# Motivational Interviewing

Motivational Interviewing (MI) is a collaborative, goal-oriented communication style focused on the language of change—designed to strengthen motivation and commitment by eliciting a person’s own reasons for change within an atmosphere of acceptance and compassion.

---

## When MI is useful

From [[motivational-interviewing]]:\n
- ambivalence is high\n
- confidence is low\n
- desire/importance is unclear\n

---

## Core elements

- **Spirit**: partnership, evocation, acceptance, compassion\n
- **Skills**: OARS (open questions, affirmations, reflections, summaries)\n
- **Processes**: engaging → focusing → evoking → planning (optional)\n
- **Information exchange**: Elicit–Provide–Elicit\n

---

## Think First relevance

MI is a structured way to help someone clarify what they actually want, what they’re resisting, and what matters—useful for moving from vague “should” goals to grounded commitment without coercion.
