# Premature Solutioning

The tendency to jump to a solution before the problem is understood—often driven by excitement, ego, or the false confidence that comes from collecting “good feedback” that is actually bad data.

---

## How it shows up in customer conversations

Premature solutioning frequently appears as:\n
- pitching too early,\n
- asking questions about *your idea* (“Would you buy…?”) rather than the customer’s reality,\n
- mistaking compliments and hypotheticals for evidence.\n

[[the-mom-test]] frames this as dangerous because it creates **false positives** that cause teams to over-invest time and money.

---

## Countermeasure

Replace solution talk with truth-seeking questions:\n
- talk about their life,\n
- ask about specific past events,\n
- watch or reconstruct the real workflow.\n
