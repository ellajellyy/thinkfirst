# Conversational AI UX

Conversational AI UX is about designing systems that can handle how people *actually* ask for help: underspecified queries, shifting goals, and diverse conversation patterns—without trapping users in brittle scripts.

---

## Core UX tensions

From [[conversational-ux-design]]:\n
- Users often begin underspecified and refine constraints over time (funneling).\n
- Some users want breadth (chiseling) while others want depth (exploring).\n
- Many bots are linear decision trees; they succeed only under compliant inputs.\n

---

## Implications for Think First

Think First workflows often require helping users clarify problems. Conversational UX should therefore:\n
- ask “helping questions” to surface constraints,\n
- support progressive disclosure,\n
- and provide explicit escape hatches (handoff to human / alternative channel) when ambiguity or risk is high.\n
