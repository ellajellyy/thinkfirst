# User Research Methods

Methods for learning how people behave, what they’re trying to accomplish, and what constraints they operate under—so you can define the real problem before committing to a solution.

---

## Customer interviews (truth-seeking)

[[the-mom-test]] provides a practical interviewing method:\n
- Focus on past behavior and concrete examples.\n
- Avoid opinions, hypotheticals, and “future promises.”\n
- Prefer walkthroughs of the last time something happened.\n
- Seek commitments (time, money, intros) as stronger evidence than enthusiasm.\n

---

## Why it matters for Think First

Good user research is one of the primary ways Think First practitioners can **convert uncertainty into understanding**—before they build, hire, or ship.
